The Challenge

Saving Lives Through
Intelligent Allocation

Every day, biological resources expire unused in one hospital while patients die waiting at another. VitalChain addresses this by training AI agents on the hardest logistics problem in medicine.

Multi-Hospital Networks

Coordinate resource allocation across 1 to 5 hospitals with real transport delays and logistics constraints.

Real Expiry Clocks

Platelets expire in 5 days. Hearts in 5 hours. Every tick of the clock matters. No room for waste.

ABO / HLA Matching

Enforce real-world blood type compatibility and HLA tissue matching for bone marrow transplants.

Patient Equity

Prevent resource monopolisation. Ensure fair distribution across all hospitals in the network.

What We Manage

Beyond Blood.
Every Transplantable Biologic.

VitalChain isn't limited to blood. It manages the complete spectrum of transplantable biological resources — each with unique constraints, shelf lives, and compatibility rules.

Heart

4–6 hour window ABO match required

The most time-critical organ. Cold ischemia time of 4–6 hours demands rapid AI decision-making for optimal recipient matching.

Kidneys

36 hour window ABO match required

The most commonly transplanted organ. VitalChain optimises for distance, compatibility, and patient urgency across the network.

Liver

24 hour window Partial transplant possible

Living-donor partial hepatectomy adds complexity. The AI must weigh split-liver options against full transplant availability.

Bone Marrow

48 hour window HLA matching required

Requires precise 6-locus HLA matching. A perfect match can mean the difference between engraftment and rejection.

The Pipeline

How VitalChain Learns

A curriculum-based reinforcement learning pipeline that progressively challenges the AI — from single-hospital blood banks to multi-site mass casualty response.

1
Stage 1 — Easy

Blood Bank Manager

Single hospital. RBC only. O+ blood type. No expiry pressure. Master the basics of allocation before complexity scales.

Patient Outcome

+0.5 to +5.0 for treating patients based on urgency. -5.0 for deaths. The primary learning signal.
